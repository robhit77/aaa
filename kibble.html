Help Feed Shelter Animals

Want to donate to animal shelters but short on cash? Play the kibble game — a free way to help feed shelter animals on California's Central Coast. Answer questions, and every correct answer helps fund a donation for the dogs and cats waiting for homes. No donation required; just play.

Every animal in a shelter needs food, medical care, and time before it finds a family — and all of that costs money a local nonprofit has to raise. Playing here is a free way to help animal shelters even if you can't adopt or write a check right now. It's Central Coast animal rescue you can support from your couch.

Your play supports Santa Barbara Humane, one of the oldest animal-welfare organizations in the West — founded in 1887 — caring for dogs and cats at its campuses in Santa Maria and Santa Barbara through adoptions, low-cost veterinary care, vaccinations, and dog training. It's a local nonprofit, not funded or controlled by any national group. We donate to Santa Barbara Humane; it is not affiliated with, and does not sponsor or endorse, Free Aid for Answers.

Answer financial literacy questions or general knowledge — either way, you help rescue animals in California, for free.
